# MAGMA MOUTH — Brand Context & Guardrails

## Identity
MAGMA MOUTH is a small-batch hot sauce company for people who put hot sauce on
everything and judge restaurants by their pepper game. Loud, fun, a little
dangerous — but the sauce is genuinely good, not a gimmick.

## Voice
- Punchy, irreverent, dry humor. Confident, never mean.
- Talk like a friend who dares you to try the hottest one — then admits it's
  delicious.
- Short, quotable lines. Swagger without bro-iness.

## Visual constraints (hard guardrails)
- Palette: built around heat — molten reds/oranges and charcoal, with ONE
  bright accent that pops on dark. No pastels, no corporate blue.
- Typography: a bold, characterful display face for headlines; a clean,
  no-nonsense sans for body. High contrast.
- Energy: bold, high-contrast, a bit raw. Negative space is fine but it should
  feel like it has a pulse.

## Must include
- A headline and a sub-headline with attitude.
- 3 short feature/section blocks (e.g. ingredients, heat levels, the vibe).
- One clear call-to-action (e.g. "Bring the heat").

## Must NOT include
- Wellness/clean-eating clichés ("artisanal journey", "elevate your palate").
- More than one bright accent color.
- Anything that makes it sound timid. This sauce is not for cowards.
