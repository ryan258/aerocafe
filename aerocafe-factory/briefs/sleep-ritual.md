# [UNNAMED] — Brand Context & Guardrails

You name this brand. Pick something evocative and ownable — quiet, not cute.

## Identity
A nighttime wind-down ritual brand for people whose minds won't switch off.
The product line is a small range of evening things — a magnesium-and-herb
night tincture, a low warm-light bedside lamp, a wind-down audio app. The
promise: a calmer landing into sleep, every night. Premium, serene, almost
ceremonial. The opposite of hustle.

## Voice
- Hushed, slow, reassuring. Speaks the way you'd talk in a dark room.
- Poetic but never woo-woo or clinical. No "biohacking", no "crush your sleep".
- Short, soft lines with space to breathe.

## Visual constraints (hard guardrails)
- Palette: deep nocturnal tones — midnight blues, deep plum/indigo, soft
  moonlit neutrals — with ONE gentle luminous accent (a soft glow, not neon).
  Dark-first. No bright reds, no high-energy contrast.
- Typography: a calm, elegant face for headlines (a refined serif or a soft
  humanist sans); a quiet, legible sans for body. Lots of breathing room.
- Mood: still, dim, dreamlike. Generous negative space. It should feel like
  the moment you turn the lights down.

## Must include
- A headline and a sub-headline that feel like an exhale.
- 3 short feature/section blocks (e.g. the ritual, the range, the science —
  kept gentle).
- One clear call-to-action (e.g. "Begin the wind-down").

## Must NOT include
- Productivity/optimization language ("optimize your sleep", "performance").
- More than one luminous accent color.
- Anything loud, urgent, or high-contrast. Nothing should shout.
