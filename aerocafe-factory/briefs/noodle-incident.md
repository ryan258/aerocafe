# THE NOODLE INCIDENT — Brand Context & Guardrails

## Identity
THE NOODLE INCIDENT is an instant-ramen brand that treats every cup like an
unsolved mystery. Absurdist, deadpan, gleefully unhinged — a late-night snack
that acts like it knows something you don't. The noodles are genuinely good;
the brand just refuses to explain itself.

## Voice
- Deadpan-absurd. States ridiculous things as established fact.
- Conspiratorial but harmless: "We do not discuss the broth." "Flavor #7 has
  never been recovered."
- Short, clipped, weirdly confident. Dry humor, never zany or exclamation-heavy.

## Visual constraints (hard guardrails)
- Wonky on purpose: off-kilter, slightly chaotic energy — but still legible and
  organized. Controlled chaos, not a mess.
- Palette: punchy and a little off — a DARK, high-contrast base (deep near-black
  or a deep saturated tone) with ONE electric accent that pops loudly against it.
  The accent must stay clearly legible on the base (no bright-on-bright). No
  tasteful muted minimalism — loud, but dark-anchored.
- Typography: a bold, characterful, almost-too-much display face for headlines;
  a clean grotesque sans for body so the wonk stays readable.
- Energy: playful, surreal, off-balance. Tilt and asymmetry welcome.

## Must include
- A headline and a sub-headline that raise more questions than they answer.
- 3 short feature/section blocks (e.g. the broth, the flavors, the incident).
- One clear call-to-action (e.g. "Open a cup. Tell no one.").

## Must NOT include
- Earnest foodie language ("crafted with love", "wholesome", "comfort food").
- More than one bright accent color.
- Anything that explains the joke. Never wink at the camera.
